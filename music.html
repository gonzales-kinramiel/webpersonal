My Beat

Rock

Rock became my favorite genre of music because this hyped me up everytime, even if i'm coding, training, doing chores, and my pre-game songs

    Sweet child of Mine by Guns and Roses

    "Sweet Child O' Mine" by Guns N' Roses is a love song written by Axl Rose for his then-girlfriend, Erin Everly. The lyrics express deep admiration and nostalgia, comparing her beauty and presence to childhood innocence. Slash originally played the iconic riff as a joke, but the band turned it into one of their biggest hits. As the song progresses, it shifts from love and warmth to a sense of uncertainty and fear of loss. Despite its romantic origins, it has become a universal rock anthem about love, memories, and longing.

    Bohemian Rhapsody by Queen

    "Bohemian Rhapsody" by Queen is a six-minute rock opera that tells a dramatic, ambiguous story. The lyrics follow a young man who confesses to committing a murder, faces guilt, and experiences a surreal journey through judgment and despair. Freddie Mercury never fully explained the song's meaning, leading to theories that it reflects personal struggles, a fictional narrative, or even hidden symbolism. Its mix of ballad, opera, hard rock, and progressive elements makes it one of the most unique songs in music history. Ultimately, the song's emotional depth and musical brilliance have made it an enduring classic.

    Basket Case by Green Day

    "Basket Case" by Green Day is a song about anxiety, paranoia, and mental struggles. Billie Joe Armstrong wrote it about his own experiences with panic attacks before being diagnosed with anxiety disorder. The lyrics describe feelings of confusion and frustration, questioning whether he's losing his mind. Despite its serious theme, the upbeat punk-rock energy makes it relatable and cathartic for many listeners. The song became one of Green Day's biggest hits, symbolizing teenage angst and emotional turmoil.

    Same Ground by Kitchie Nadal

    "Same Ground" by Kitchie Nadal is a heartfelt song about love, loss, and moving on. The lyrics reflect on a past relationship where both partners have grown apart despite once being deeply in love. It conveys the pain of realizing that no matter how much effort is made, some relationships are not meant to last. The song's melancholic tone and emotional lyrics resonate with anyone who has experienced heartbreak. Ultimately, it speaks about acceptance and finding the strength to let go while cherishing the memories.

    Jopay by Mayonaise

    "Jopay" by Mayonnaise is a song about longing, unrequited love, and nostalgia. The lyrics express deep admiration and yearning for someone, possibly inspired by Jopay Paguia, a former SexBomb dancer. It captures the feelings of a person reminiscing about past moments and hoping for another chance. The song's melancholic yet catchy melody has made it a timeless OPM hit. Over the years, it has gained a resurgence in popularity, resonating with both old and new listeners.

    Elesi by Rivermaya

    "Elesi" by Rivermaya is a song about resilience and hope amid life's struggles. The lyrics use rain and storms as metaphors for challenges, encouraging the listener to keep going despite difficulties. It conveys a message that hardships are temporary, and brighter days will come. The song’s uplifting yet slightly melancholic tone resonates with those facing personal battles. Ultimately, Elesi reminds listeners that no storm lasts forever, and perseverance leads to better days.

    Feeling Good by Michael Buble

    "Feeling Good" by Michael Bublé (originally sung by Nina Simone) is a song about renewal, freedom, and optimism. The lyrics use nature imagery—like birds flying and rivers flowing—to symbolize a fresh start and a sense of liberation. It conveys confidence and empowerment, as if leaving behind past struggles and embracing a brighter future. Bublé's version adds a smooth, jazzy touch that enhances the song's uplifting mood. Overall, it's an anthem of self-assurance and embracing life with a renewed perspective.

    What a wonderful world by Louis Armstrong

    "What a Wonderful World" by Louis Armstrong is a song about appreciating the beauty of life and the world around us. The lyrics describe simple yet meaningful moments, like blue skies, green trees, and friendly smiles, highlighting the goodness in everyday life. Released during a time of social unrest, the song served as a reminder of hope, peace, and optimism. Armstrong's warm and heartfelt delivery makes the message even more powerful. Ultimately, the song encourages gratitude and a positive outlook despite life's challenges.

    Fly me to the Moon by Frank Sinatraa

    "Fly Me to the Moon" by Frank Sinatra is a romantic jazz song expressing deep love and admiration. The lyrics use space exploration as a metaphor for an exciting and limitless love, asking to be taken beyond Earth’s boundaries. Sinatra's smooth vocals and the song's elegant orchestration make it a timeless classic. It became closely associated with NASA's Apollo missions, symbolizing human ambition and dreams. Ultimately, the song captures the feeling of love as an extraordinary and boundless journey.
